## Abstract

The **79 Invariants for Intelligibility** (also known as the **Conditions for the Possibility of Everything — CFPE**) constitute the **transcendental conditions** that must hold for any coherent world, system, or domain of existence.

Following Addendum v1.2's reconceptualization, these conditions are stratified:
- **3 Universal Invariants** (C₁, C₂, C₃): Necessary across all formal systems
- **76 Contextual Invariants**: Necessary for systems with specific properties (temporal, spatial, epistemic, phenomenological, normative)

This represents a **honest scope limitation** compared to v1.1's claim of universal necessity for all 79 conditions.

---

## I. The 10 Categories of Invariants

The 79 invariants are organized into 10 irreducible categories, each addressing a fundamental dimension of coherence:

1. **Ontological** (C₁–C₁₀): Being, identity, persistence, transformation
2. **Logical-Formal** (C₁₁–C₂₀): Inference, consistency, metabolic non-contradiction
3. **Temporal-Dynamical** (C₂₁–C₃₀): Time, causality, recursion, emergence
4. **Relational-Structural** (C₃₁–C₄₀): Space, networks, hierarchy, boundaries
5. **Epistemic-Cognitive** (C₄₁–C₅₀): Knowledge, observation, intelligibility
6. **Semantic-Linguistic** (C₅₁–C₆₀): Meaning, reference, communication
7. **Normative-Ethical** (C₆₁–C₆₈): Value, agency, justice, generativity
8. **Modal-Counterfactual** (C₆₉–C₇₂): Necessity, possibility, contingency
9. **Existential-Phenomenological** (C₇₃–C₇₆): Experience, embodiment, affect
10. **Systemic-Integrative** (C₇₇–C₇₉): Self-organization, evolution, architectural bloom

## II. Category I: Ontological Conditions (C₁–C₁₀)

These conditions establish the fundamental structure of being.

### C₁: Existence
**Status:** Universal Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \Lambda : \Lambda \neq \emptyset$  
**Formal English:** Something exists rather than nothing.

**Elaboration:**
The most fundamental condition asserts sheer ontological presence. This is not a commitment to any particular thing, but to the *possibility of presence itself*.

**Performative Necessity:**
To deny existence requires language, concepts, a subject—each of which *is* existence. The denial presupposes what it rejects.

**Addendum v1.2:** This maps to Axiom A₂ (Identity at symbol level).

---

### C₂: Coherence
**Status:** Universal Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x \in \Lambda : \text{Coherent}(x)$  
**Formal English:** All existent entities maintain internal consistency.

**Elaboration:**
Coherence is the binding principle that holds entities together. Without coherence, entities dissolve into contradictory non-being.

**Addendum v1.2:** This maps to Axiom A₁ (Consistency).

---

### C₃: Identity
**Status:** Universal Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x (x = x)$  
**Formal English:** Every entity is identical to itself.

**Elaboration:**
Identity is the foundational logical principle enabling distinctions and reference. Without identity, no proposition can be made about anything.

**Addendum v1.2:** This is Axiom A₂ (Identity) extended to ontological domain.

---

### C₄: Difference
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists x, y (x \neq y)$  
**Formal English:** At least two distinct entities exist.

**Elaboration:**
Difference is the correlate of identity. Without otherness, identity becomes vacuous—indistinguishable from non-being.

**Applicability:** Systems with multiplicity (not singleton universes).

---

### C₅: Persistence
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x \in \Lambda, \exists t_1, t_2 (t_1 < t_2 \to x(t_1) \approx x(t_2))$  
**Formal English:** Entities maintain recognizable continuity across time.

**Elaboration:**
Persistence is the temporal dimension of identity. Without persistence, there is no time—only instantaneous moments bearing no relation.

**Applicability:** Temporal systems (not eternal/static systems).

---

### C₆: Transformability
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \delta : \Lambda \to \Lambda, \delta \neq \text{id}$  
**Formal English:** Non-trivial transformations are possible.

**Elaboration:**
Change is ontologically real, not merely epistemic illusion. The substrate generates non-trivial transformations.

**Applicability:** Dynamic systems (not static structures).

---

### C₇: Potentiality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x \in \Lambda : \exists p \in \text{Potentials}(x)$  
**Formal English:** Entities possess unrealized possibilities.

**Elaboration:**
Potentiality is the substrate of becoming. Entities are not exhausted by their current state but contain latent capacities.

**Applicability:** Modal systems admitting possible states.

---

### C₈: Constraint
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \text{Constraints}(S) : S \subseteq \Lambda$  
**Formal English:** Systems operate under limiting conditions.

**Elaboration:**
Constraint is not limitation but structure-enabling. Without constraints, systems dissolve into undifferentiated chaos.

**Applicability:** Structured systems (not totally unconstrained spaces).

---

### C₉: Self-Containment
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall S \subseteq \Lambda : \exists \partial S$  
**Formal English:** Systems have boundaries distinguishing interior from exterior.

**Elaboration:**
Boundaries enable systems to maintain identity and interact with environment while preserving internal coherence.

**Applicability:** Systems with defined extents.

---

### C₁₀: Individuation
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x, y \in \Lambda : \exists \text{Property}(x) \neq \text{Property}(y)$  
**Formal English:** Entities are distinguishable by their properties.

**Elaboration:**
Individuation ensures entities are not merely numerically distinct but qualitatively differentiated.

**Applicability:** Property-bearing systems.

## III. Category II: Logical-Formal Conditions (C₁₁–C₂₀)

These conditions enable meaningful inference and systematic reasoning.

### C₁₁: Identity (Logical)
**Status:** Universal Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall p : (p = p)$  
**Formal English:** Propositions are identical to themselves.

**Elaboration:**
Logical identity extends ontological identity to the domain of propositions and truth-values.

**Addendum v1.2:** Core component of Axiom A₂.

---

### C₁₂: Difference (Logical)
**Status:** Universal Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists p, q : (p \neq q)$  
**Formal English:** At least two distinct propositions exist.

**Elaboration:**
Logical difference enables meaningful propositions. If all propositions were identical, no reasoning would be possible.

**Addendum v1.2:** Core component of Axiom A₃.

---

### C₁₃: Metabolic Non-Contradiction
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\Omega_0(\phi \land \lnot \phi) = G^{\omega}$  
**Formal English:** Contradictions metabolize into generative states rather than exploding.

**Elaboration:**
Where classical logic treats contradiction as catastrophic, Generative Logic treats it as catalytic. Contradictions do not explode into triviality but metabolize into enhanced generative potential.

**Applicability:** Paraconsistent systems (not classical logic).

**Addendum v1.2:** Grounded in Axiom A₅ (Paraconsistent Non-Explosion) and A₆ (Metabolic Transformation).

---

### C₁₄: Excluded Middle (Qualified)
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall p : (p \lor \lnot p) \text{ in } D_{\text{actual}}$  
**Formal English:** In the actual domain, propositions are either true or false.

**Elaboration:**
Excluded middle holds for actualized states but not for virtual/potential domains. This allows for non-classical logics while preserving classical reasoning where appropriate.

**Applicability:** Actual (not virtual/quantum) domains.

---

### C₁₅: Compositionality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \circ : \text{Propositions} \times \text{Propositions} \to \text{Propositions}$  
**Formal English:** Propositions can be combined to form complex propositions.

**Elaboration:**
Compositionality enables systematic reasoning from simpler to more complex truths.

**Applicability:** Compositional logical systems.

---

### C₁₆: Expressivity
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall \text{State}(s) : \exists p : p \text{ expresses } s$  
**Formal English:** Every system state can be expressed propositionally.

**Elaboration:**
Expressivity ensures that reasoning can track reality—propositions are adequate to their subject matter.

**Applicability:** Linguistically expressible domains.

---

### C₁₇: Reflexivity
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall S : S \text{ can represent itself}$  
**Formal English:** Systems can self-reference.

**Elaboration:**
Reflexivity enables meta-level reasoning, self-awareness, and recursive structures.

**Applicability:** Self-referential systems.

---

### C₁₈: Closure of Inference
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall p, q : (p \to q) \land p \vdash q$  
**Formal English:** Modus ponens and logical derivation are sound.

**Elaboration:**
Inference preserves truth from premises to conclusions, making systematic reasoning reliable.

**Applicability:** Deductive systems.

---

### C₁₉: Formal Adequacy
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists L : L \text{ is sound and consistent}$  
**Formal English:** A formal language adequate to reasoning exists.

**Elaboration:**
Formal systems can be constructed that reliably track truth without internal contradiction.

**Applicability:** Formalizable domains.

---

### C₂₀: Intentionality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall \text{Representation}(r) : r \text{ points-to } \text{target}$  
**Formal English:** Representations are about something.

**Elaboration:**
Intentionality grounds reference—the "aboutness" that makes propositions meaningful rather than mere syntax.

**Applicability:** Semantic/intentional systems.

## IV. Category III: Temporal-Dynamical Conditions (C₂₁–C₃₀)

These conditions enable time, causality, and change.

### C₂₁: Temporality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists ≺ : \text{Events} \to \text{Events}$  
**Formal English:** Events admit temporal ordering.

**Elaboration:**
Time is not merely parameter but structure—events stand in earlier/later relations.

**Applicability:** Temporal systems (not eternal/atemporal domains).

**Addendum v1.2:** Grounded in Axiom A₄ (Temporal Ordering).

---

### C₂₂: Causality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall e_1, e_2 : (e_1 \to e_2) \land (e_2 \rightsquigarrow e_1)$  
**Formal English:** Events exhibit causal and feedback relations.

**Elaboration:**
Causality operates bidirectionally—effects re-inform causes, stabilizing temporal coherence.

**Applicability:** Causal systems.

---

### C₂₃: Irreversibility (Selective Eternal Return)
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists t : \text{Arrow}(t) \neq \text{reversible}$  
**Formal English:** Some processes are irreversible.

**Elaboration:**
Irreversibility grounds history and thermodynamic arrow of time while allowing for cyclic/reversible processes in specific domains.

**Applicability:** Thermodynamic/historical systems.

---

### C₂₄: Recursion
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall f : f(f(x)) \text{ is defined}$  
**Formal English:** Operations can be applied to their own outputs.

**Elaboration:**
Recursion enables self-similarity, fractality, and meta-level operations.

**Applicability:** Recursive systems.

---

### C₂₅: Memory/Retention
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall S : \exists \text{History}(S, t)$  
**Formal English:** Systems retain traces of past states.

**Elaboration:**
Memory makes learning, adaptation, and historical identity possible.

**Applicability:** Historical/learning systems.

---

### C₂₆: Anticipation/Protention
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall S : \exists \text{Forecast}(S, t+\Delta t)$  
**Formal English:** Systems project future states.

**Elaboration:**
Anticipation enables planning, prediction, and teleological structure.

**Applicability:** Predictive/goal-oriented systems.

---

### C₂₇: Continuity
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x(t) : \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} x(t+\Delta t) = x(t)$  
**Formal English:** Change is continuous (no jumps).

**Elaboration:**
Continuity ensures smooth transitions, making calculus and differential equations applicable.

**Applicability:** Continuous dynamics (not quantum/discrete systems).

---

### C₂₈: Emergence
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \text{Property}(S) : \text{Property}(S) \notin \bigcup \text{Properties}(\text{parts}(S))$  
**Formal English:** Wholes possess properties not reducible to parts.

**Elaboration:**
Emergence enables complexity—new properties arise at higher scales of organization.

**Applicability:** Complex/hierarchical systems.

---

### C₂₉: Feedback
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists \text{Loop} : \text{Output}(S) \to \text{Input}(S)$  
**Formal English:** System outputs feed back into inputs.

**Elaboration:**
Feedback enables self-regulation, homeostasis, and adaptive control.

**Applicability:** Cybernetic/regulatory systems.

---

### C₃₀: Path-Dependence
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $S(t) = f(\text{History}(S, [0, t]))$  
**Formal English:** Current state depends on historical trajectory.

**Elaboration:**
Path-dependence ensures history matters—systems carry memory of their evolution.

**Applicability:** Historical/evolutionary systems.

## V. Category IV: Relational-Structural Conditions (C₃₁–C₄₀)

These conditions enable spatial organization and networked structures.

### C₃₁: Spatiality
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists d : \Lambda \times \Lambda \to \mathbb{R}^+$  
**Formal English:** Entities admit spatial distance relations.

**Elaboration:**
Spatiality enables localization, extension, and geometric structure.

**Applicability:** Spatial systems (not purely temporal/abstract domains).

---

**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\exists g : S \to S$ where $g(S) = S$ or $g(S) \neq S$  
**Formal English:** Systems exhibit both symmetries and asymmetries.

**Elaboration:**
Symmetries indicate conservation laws; asymmetries enable differentiation and breaking of degeneracy.

**Applicability:** Structured systems.

---

**Status:** Contextual Invariant

Hierarchy enables multi-scale organization and efficient control structures.

---

### C₃₄: Network Connectivity

Networks enable information flow, influence propagation, and emergent collective behavior.

**Applicability:** Networked systems.

---

### C₃₅: Boundary Definition
**Status:** Contextual Invariant

**Elaboration:**
Boundaries distinguish system from environment while enabling interaction.

**Applicability:** Bounded systems.

---

### C₃₆: Integration
**Status:** Contextual Invariant

**Elaboration:**
Integration transforms aggregates into systems with emergent identity.

**Applicability:** Integrated systems.

### C₃₇: Modularity
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $S = \bigcup \text{Modules}_i$ where modules are semi-independent

**Elaboration:**
Modularity enables evolution, robustness, and comprehensibility.

**Applicability:** Modular/decomposable systems.

---

### C₃₈: Reciprocal Determination
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $\forall x, y : x \leftrightarrow y$  
**Formal English:** Entities mutually determine each other.

**Elaboration:**
Reciprocity ensures relations are not one-sided but mutually constitutive.

**Applicability:** Relational systems.

---

### C₃₉: Disjunctive Synthesis (Deleuzian Univocity)
**Status:** Contextual Invariant  
**Formula:** $(\forall x) [\text{Being}(x) = \text{Being}(y)] \oplus (\forall x,y) [x \neq y]$  
**Formal English:** Unity of being coexists with pervasive difference.

**Elaboration:**
Disjunctive synthesis maintains both unity and difference without resolving tension—the formal reconciliation of Deleuze with first-order logic.

**Applicability:** Λ-substrate systems.

Coupling enables interaction without collapse into identity.

**Applicability:** Interactive systems.

- Overreaching universality claims

- Acknowledgment that phenomenological/normative conditions are not derivable from logic
- Rigorous formal grounding for universal conditions

## IX. Verification via Performative Contradiction

### Test Protocol

For each condition C_i:
1. Assume ¬C_i (deny the condition)
2. Attempt to formulate the denial coherently
3. Show that formulating the denial requires presupposing C_i
4. Conclude C_i is necessary (within its domain)

### Examples

**C₁ (Existence):**
- Deny: "Nothing exists"
- Requires: Language, concepts, a denier
- Each requirement presupposes existence
- Conclusion: C₁ is necessary ∎

**C₁₃ (Metabolic Non-Contradiction):**
- Deny: "Contradictions always explode" (assert classical explosion)
- In classical logic: (φ ∧ ¬φ) → ψ for all ψ
- Result: All propositions become true (triviality)
- System becomes incoherent, unintelligible
- Conclusion: C₁₃ is necessary for paraconsistent systems ∎

**C₇₃ (Givenness - Phenomenological):**
- Deny: "Nothing is given in experience"
- Requires: Experiential capacity to formulate denial
- Experience presupposes givenness
- Conclusion: C₇₃ is necessary for phenomenological domains ∎
